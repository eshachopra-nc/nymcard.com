## §2. The pain

**Headline**
A BNPL decision has milliseconds to be right.

**Support**
The offer has to land at checkout, the decision has to clear in real time, and the loan has to be serviced for months afterward. Most BNPL stacks split that across a decisioning vendor, a servicing system, and a separate way to actually fund the spend.

**Three pains:**

1. **The decision arrives too late.** Underwriting that runs outside the checkout adds latency and friction exactly where conversion is won or lost.

2. **Approval and spend are disconnected.** Approving credit is one system; letting the customer actually spend it is another, so a green light doesn't translate into a usable line.

3. **Servicing is someone else's problem.** Repayments, billing cycles, and collections sit in a separate platform, leaving you with a portfolio you can't see end to end.

---

## §3. The shift

**Headline**
Decisioning, origination, and the card on one core.

**Body**
nCore runs credit decisioning, origination, and servicing on the same platform that issues the card the customer spends on. The decision and the spend share one customer record, so an approval at checkout becomes a usable line in real time.

**What changes:**

1. **Decisions in the flow.** Underwriting runs inside the checkout path, not as a downstream call.

2. **Approval becomes spend.** A decision provisions a virtual or physical card instantly, so credit is immediately usable.

3. **One portfolio view.** Origination, servicing, and repayment live on one core, so you see the whole book in one place.

## §4. What it bundles

**Headline**
Two layers, one checkout-to-repayment loop.

**Body**
BNPL is a tight loop: approve in the moment, let the customer spend, then service the loan to term. Two nCore layers close that loop without a handoff.

- **Lending:** Run real-time credit decisioning, origination, and servicing across installment and revolving plans, with NymCard providing the infrastructure while you set the offer and keep the relationship.

- **Cards:** Turn an approval into a usable line the instant it clears, by provisioning a virtual or physical card the customer spends on directly.
