# NymCard design system evaluation — 2026-05-25

## Verdict in one paragraph

NymCard's design system is **structurally further along than visually finished**. The plumbing — tokens, the 22-primitive catalogue in §8.x, the visual-system styleguide, the cool-only palette discipline, the new representative product UIs — is genuinely good, in some places (CodeArtifact, AI extraction scan-sweep spec, the consolidated CardGrid, the page-arc-aligned section primitives) operating at unicorn-adjacent bar. The visible site, however, does not yet earn that bar. Three things hold it back, in this order: (1) the bespoke product UIs and infographics that carry the storytelling are largely placeholder-grade compared to Stripe's commissioned UI vignettes; (2) the typeface choice (Satoshi + Inter) is competent but generic and does not differentiate NymCard from any other 2024-vintage SaaS — Anthropic, Linear, Vercel all earn distinctiveness partly through type; (3) the colour palette is so disciplined-cool that NymCard has no recognisable signature gradient or accent moment — Stripe is purple-coral-cyan, Linear is purple, Vercel is monochrome-with-restraint; NymCard is "blue infrastructure brand #47." The system is one strong push from world-class. Today it reads as on-system but undifferentiated.

## Where the system is already strong

- **The §8.x catalogue itself is dense and reference-aligned.** 22 primitives covering composition, surfaces, code, rails, hero, page-arc industry templates — every section a marketing page wants to render is there, with a Stripe / Linear / Vercel reference per primitive. That body of decisions is good.
- **CardGrid consolidation (v1.7).** Six overlapping grids collapsed to one prop-configured `CardGrid` (`layout × card-type × surface`). This is exactly the kind of consolidation a mature system does — and most teams never get to.
- **CodeArtifact (§8.17).** The cyan-on-navy tab strip, IBM Plex Mono line-numbered body, restrained cool-palette syntax tokens with the small companion block beneath — this is on-bar with Stripe Issuing's developer panel. The token-level discipline (no rainbow highlighter, no inline hex) is right.
- **AI-native data extraction motion (§9.5.1).** The three-layer scan ripple (halo + cyan band + trailing shadow), surface materialisation (scale + drift + blur fade together), staggered chip reveals with parallax scale-delta — this is the system's strongest single moment and is the closest thing to a real differentiator.
- **Cool-only discipline.** The v1.2 decision to remove warm accents is correct and unusual — most fintech brands cave to the warm gradient. The discipline gives you something to stand for visually. (The trade-off is in the recognisability gap, below — but the discipline itself is right.)
- **Theme model.** First-class light AND dark, with `prefers-color-scheme` + persisted toggle, every primitive dual-rendered. Linear-grade. Many competitors fake one or the other.
- **Page rails + radius enforcement.** The full-document vertical rails + horizontal rail below the nav, plus the strict eight-radius scale with grep-on-PR enforcement, are real continuity work. The page reads as one canvas.

## The gaps — by dimension

### 1. Typography
**Current state.** Satoshi 700 for display, Inter 400/500/600 for body, IBM Plex Mono for code. The scale (`display-xl` 72/80, `h2` 36/44, `body` 16/26) is correctly proportioned and the rules (no italics, no all-caps, no eyebrow style, tighter tracking at display sizes) are well-formed. Headline weight rhythm is right — Satoshi 700 at display, 500 for h3/h4 — the kind of differentiation a serious system gets right. **But Satoshi + Inter is the most-used premium-SaaS pairing of 2024–25.** Stitch, Stripe-clones, every "we want to look serious" template defaults here. NymCard's typography is correct and forgettable in the same breath.

**Reference move NymCard doesn't have.** Anthropic uses Söhne — a Lineto licence that bills mid-four-figures per year and reads as institutional editorial. Vercel uses Geist, a typeface they shipped themselves and which is now visually synonymous with Vercel — a moat made of glyphs. Linear uses Inter Display (Inter's tighter display sister) at very tight letter-spacing for the largest sizes — a small specifics-driven differentiation. See `linear-hero.png` for the tracking discipline.

**Highest-impact change.** Adopt a distinctive display typeface for the hero and h1/h2 only — keep Inter for body. Options: ABC Diatype, Söhne (license), GT Eesti Display, Telegraf, Geist (free), or commission a slight Inter Display variant. Anything that visually distinguishes the headline from "another B2B page." Cost: license / token swap.

### 2. Colour system
**Current state.** Brand blue (`#304DBB`), brand purple (`#5B4FD9`), navy (`#0E1A33`), accent cyan / teal / indigo. Semantic success/warning/danger/info present. Three documented gradients (hero kinetic, section ambient, dark kinetic ribbon). Cool-only enforced. The discipline is real — but the result is that the page reads as monochromatic-blue. The hero kinetic gradient is the only place a buyer sees colour-as-identity, and even that gradient is so tonally similar to a hundred other "blue-purple-cyan" brand washes that it doesn't read as NymCard's.

**Reference move NymCard doesn't have.** Stripe has *coral-pink-purple-violet* as a vibrating signature wash (`stripe-home-hero.png`, `stripe-product-home.png`) that you'd recognise from a fingernail-sized crop. Linear has the purple-on-near-black that's almost gimballed to the brand. Anthropic doesn't have a signature gradient at all — it has signature *warm neutrals* (cream, terracotta clay, slate) that are equally identifiable. NymCard currently has *none of the three patterns*.

**Highest-impact change.** Introduce one cool-but-distinct **signature accent**: either a single deeper-violet or electric-azure that's owned by NymCard (and used like Linear uses its purple — sparingly, in the hero, the primary CTA hover, one focal accent per section), or a signature gradient stop combination that is not "cyan → purple → blue." A possible move: add a fourth cool token in the deep-teal / iridescent green-cyan space (still cool, still on-brand) and use it as a single recognisable accent moment.

### 3. Spacing, rhythm, section design
**Current state.** Section padding rules are explicit (hero/dark/final-cta at `space-12` = 120px, standard at `space-11` = 96px). 4px base, no magic numbers. Grid: 12-col with 24px gutter, container 1200/1440. This is the strongest unsexy dimension — it is correctly specified and rendered. Section transitions, however, are largely "next section pad bottom + next section pad top," which is generic. There's a `light → dark` and `dark → light` transition rule with a 40–80px gradient or diagonal slash, but in practice the homepage today reads as stacked blocks with the rails papering over the seams.

**Reference move NymCard doesn't have.** Stripe's homepage uses a deliberate **gradient bridge between sections** (you can see it where the hero pink-orange washes into the next light section) — section transitions are art-directed, not generic. Vercel uses **dotted/dashed `+` crosshair markers at column intersections** (visible in `vercel-asymetric.png`) that signal "engineered layout" — a small but distinctive editorial signature. Anthropic uses very loud vertical-line rules ("rails on steroids") between editorial blocks.

**Highest-impact change.** Either commit to the gradient-bridge transitions the system already names but does not yet ship visibly on the homepage, or adopt a Vercel-style intersection-crosshair marker as part of the page rails — turn the rails from passive ambient into an active signature.

### 4. Motion system
**Current state.** Three-category motion (ambient / kinetic / responsive), nine timing tokens, five easings, per-section motion budget. The kinetic ribbon engine is genuinely good — the homepage hero kinetic ribbon, the SplitEditorial / KineticRibbon presets (`calm` / `ambient` / `peak`, three focus positions, six environmental layers including grain). The AI extraction sub-pattern is the strongest. **However** — on the live homepage today, the motion you actually see is largely the ribbon drift and the carousel rotation; the kinetic AI moments don't fire because the product UIs are still mostly placeholder. The system *can* do the work; the page does not yet show it.

**Reference move NymCard doesn't have.** Linear's product UIs do tied-to-scroll micro-state changes — boards reorganise, threads animate, agents respond — as you read. Stripe's hover-state product UIs (`motion-stripe-floating-card-hover-state.mov`) — discrete, contained, expressive on a single hover. NymCard's product cards have a `group-hover` lift but no real demonstrable state change.

**Highest-impact change.** Lock the AI extraction sub-pattern onto two specific homepage moments — Financial Crime (KYC scan) and Reconciliation (matching scan) — and ship those product UIs at the §9.5.1 spec level. One moment of real intelligence-motion on the homepage will do more for the AI-native positioning than ten ribbon refinements.

### 5. Component primitives (§8.x catalogue)
**Current state.** 22 primitives. Strong: CardGrid, CodeArtifact, RailCarousel, SplitEditorial (at hero scale), PageHero, FAQ with JSON-LD, OutcomeChips, PlatformChecklist, DeveloperBlock, CrossSellBanner. Acceptable but generic: UIPlaceholder, FeatureCard, ProductSpotlight, FloatingOperationalPanel, DenseCapabilityCard. Notable gap: there is **no proof-of-scale section primitive** (the Stripe stats dark-mode pattern is referenced in the doc but no §8.x primitive owns it). There is also no **infrastructure-diagram primitive** at the scale of `stripe-integrations.png` — `InfraDiagramFrame` and `NCoreStack` are too small / too schematic to carry an architecture story. There is also no **logo wall / trust band** primitive beyond the existing `TrustBar` (which is page-specific, not a system primitive).

**Reference move NymCard doesn't have.** Stripe ships an explicit "Integrations" pattern (`stripe-integrations.png`) — a dark section, a node-network diagram with bordered chips connected by dotted lines into a central Stripe block, in cyan-purple. The closest NymCard primitive (NCoreStack) is a vertical layer stack — a different geometry, not a network diagram. The system is missing the "X connects to all of Y" composition entirely.

**Highest-impact change.** Add three primitives: (1) a `ScaleStatsRibbon` for the dark scale-stats moment with number count-up and dark kinetic ribbon background (the spec exists at §9.5 but no primitive carries it); (2) an `IntegrationsDiagram` for the architecture/connectors story (Stripe-style); (3) elevate `TrustBar` from a page-specific section to a system primitive with documented logo-wall behaviour.

### 6. Product illustrations and graphical elements
**This is the biggest visual gap.** Stripe ships fully bespoke product UIs for every product (the Atlas company-creation flow, the Issuing card-controls dashboard, the Connect onboarding form). They are designed surfaces, not screenshots, and they carry the page. NymCard has six homepage product UIs now (`Cards`, `Lending`, `Money Movement`, `Settlement`, `Financial Crime`, `Reconciliation`) and most still read as "lite vignette" — `CardsUI` is a card with a chip and Visa logo on a tonal gradient; `LendingUI` is a "Pay in 4" mini-widget. They are correct, on-system, faithful to placeholder rules — but compared to `stripe-added-capabilities.png` or `stripe-use-cases.png`, they are not yet at the level of detail and craft that says "this company knows what it builds."

There is **no infographic / architectural illustration** in the homepage today that does the storytelling job. `NCoreStack` is functional but visually too modest for the nCore moment — it reads as a 200×400 widget when the spec asks for a layered infrastructure illustration that anchors the section.

**Reference move NymCard doesn't have.** Stripe `stripe-money-movement.png` (the dotted-stipple globe with stablecoin flows visualised as particle density); `stripe-platform-spokes.png` (the radial spokes diagram). Linear `linear-floating-dashboard.png` (the layered glass UI carrying the whole "Self-driving operations" headline). Vercel `vercel-asymetric.png` (the literal anomaly chart with bullet-list root-cause analysis card to its right — a fake-looking but credible product moment).

**Highest-impact change.** Commission a 6-piece bespoke product UI set at Stripe parity (one per product), plus one editorial infrastructure illustration for the nCore moment (the layered platform reveal, art-directed not schematic). Until that lands, the homepage will feel one tier below Stripe regardless of how good the rest of the system is. This is the largest cost item but the single biggest visible quality lever.

### 7. Atmosphere & depth
**Current state.** `KineticRibbon` (six-layer environmental atmosphere, three intensities, three focus positions). `AmbientGlow`, `BlueprintOverlay`, `TopologyTraces`, `ScanSweep`, `SectionAtmosphere`, `SpotlightCard`, `TonalDepth`. Glass surfaces with backdrop-blur + saturation. Cyan top-edge hairline on embedded UI surfaces. **This dimension is doing real work.** The hero kinetic ribbon is genuinely premium when it lands; the per-section `KineticRibbon intensity="calm" focus="bottom-right"` system is intelligent. But — risk — the atmosphere is *louder* than the content right now. The homepage hero is mostly ribbon-and-carousel; the actual headline doesn't yet have the weight that the atmosphere implies it should.

**Reference move NymCard doesn't have.** Anthropic's homepage is *almost devoid of atmosphere* — restraint is the move. Linear uses *one* deep glow per section, sparingly, and the product UI does the rest. Vercel uses *negative space* as atmosphere. NymCard's atmosphere is closer to "always on" — every section gets a ribbon, every card gets a kinetic ribbon, every glass surface gets a cyan hairline. The risk is the same-y reading.

**Highest-impact change.** Dial back atmosphere in two of the homepage's middle sections — let Products and one of the cross-link sections sit on `surface-soft` with no ribbon and no kinetic. The peaks (hero, scale section, final CTA) will read much louder by contrast.

### 8. AI-native signals
**Current state.** The architecture document positions AI as woven into every layer (decisioning, routing, fraud, reconciliation). The design system codifies §9.5.1 — the AI extraction motion — as the one place that signal appears visually. On the live homepage, **AI is essentially invisible**. The hero copy says "AI-native platform" but no element on the page demonstrates that claim. The ticker is a counter. The carousel is product names. The product UIs are static-state representations of normal product behaviour. Nothing signals intelligence.

**Reference move NymCard doesn't have.** Anthropic has the Claude product UI itself — the AI is the product, so the page can just show the product. Linear's "agents" page (`linear.app/agents`) demonstrates AI work in literal in-product motion. Brex's invoice-scanning visual (`03-references/brex/brex-invoice-scanning.png`) — already cited as inspiration for §9.5.1 — is the kind of agent-doing-work moment NymCard hasn't yet built.

**Highest-impact change.** Ship one concrete AI moment on the homepage at §9.5.1 spec quality: a Financial Crime UI that visibly scans a transaction and extracts risk-signal chips, or a Reconciliation UI that visibly matches a multi-currency transaction set. One moment, on the page, doing the work — not in a placeholder, not in the styleguide.

### 9. Information density
**Current state.** The copy is admirably restrained — Homepage.md is short, declarative, structured. Headlines are short ("Full-stack payments infrastructure."), subheads single sentences. The system supports this — `SplitEditorial` constrains body text to `max-w-sm`, `RailCarousel` cards constrain to 3-bullet density, FAQ accordions hide secondary content. **However**, because the product UIs aren't yet doing storytelling work (point 6), the page is currently *low* in information density — the eye reads the headline, the subhead, the three CTAs, and then drops into product cards that don't say much. Stripe's homepage rewards a reading pass; NymCard's doesn't yet.

**Reference move NymCard doesn't have.** Stripe `stripe-use-cases.png` — each card is a complete product moment with 3-bullet payoff and a real-looking UI in the top half. The cards earn their place; the user reads the cards instead of just scrolling past.

**Highest-impact change.** Tie information density to product-UI density. Once the product UIs are at Stripe parity, each card on the Products grid will carry a one-sentence headline + three bullets + a real product UI — that's the right density, the system already supports it, the copy file just needs the three bullets per product.

### 10. Distinctiveness
**Current state.** This is the deepest gap. If a stranger landed on nymcard.com today and the logo were removed, would they recognise it as NymCard? Answer: probably not. They'd recognise it as "a competent payments infrastructure marketing site, vintage 2025, made by someone who studied Stripe carefully." There is no NymCard moment — no signature gradient, no signature illustration style, no signature typographic move, no signature motion that's *theirs*. The system is so disciplined it has eliminated everything that would let a brand emerge.

**Reference move NymCard doesn't have.** Stripe has the coral-purple gradient + the angled-page-edge slash (`stripe-developer-docs.png`). Linear has the cursor-trail / starfield-on-near-black + the very tight Inter Display + the purple-only accent. Anthropic has the warm-neutral palette + the Söhne wordmark + a particular brand of "almost-no-atmosphere" restraint. Vercel has Geist + monochrome-with-vivid-deployments + the crosshair-at-intersection markers. Each has at least three things you'd recognise from a thumbnail.

**Highest-impact change.** Pick **two** signature moves and lock them. Candidates: (1) a distinctive display typeface (point 1); (2) the cyan front-edge hairline + horizontal rails as a visible-not-hidden signature treatment; (3) a specific layered-stack illustration style for product visuals (the nCore-stack vocabulary, executed beautifully and consistently); (4) the AI extraction scan-sweep as the kinetic moment that says "NymCard." Two of these, executed at unicorn craft, will read as identity.

## Prioritized recommendations

1. **Commission bespoke product UIs for the six homepage products at Stripe parity.** (Largest single quality lever.) The current product UIs are correct but visually thin compared to `stripe-use-cases.png` / `stripe-added-capabilities.png`. Replace `CardsUI`, `LendingUI`, `MoneyMovementUI`, `SettlementUI`, `FinancialCrimeUI`, `ReconciliationUI` with designed product surfaces — each with 8–12 distinct UI elements, real-looking placeholder data, the cyan front-edge hairline, one ambient motion per the §8.8 spec. **Why it matters:** Product UIs carry 80% of storytelling weight (design-system.md §1.5). They are the difference between "infrastructure brand" and "unicorn infrastructure brand." **Cost:** large (6 commissioned UIs, ~3-week design sprint). **Order: 1.**

2. **Ship the AI extraction motion on the Financial Crime product UI at §9.5.1 spec.** The system already has the spec; nothing on the live homepage uses it. The single highest-impact AI-native signal we could land. **Why it matters:** "AI-native platform" is asserted in copy but invisible on the page. One real demo moment closes that gap. **Cost:** medium (one bespoke SVG with SMIL animation; the spec is already there). **Order: 2.**

3. **Adopt a distinctive display typeface for hero / h1 / h2; keep Inter for body.** Satoshi is competent and forgettable. Candidates: Geist (free), Söhne (licensed), GT Eesti Display (licensed), Telegraf (free). Update tokens, update the `font-family.display` value, regenerate. **Why it matters:** Typography is one of three things a stranger could use to recognise the brand. The current pairing actively says "templated B2B SaaS." **Cost:** small (token + license, one PR). **Order: 3.**

4. **Add a signature cool accent + recolour one moment with it.** Introduce a single deeper-violet or electric-azure as the recognisable NymCard accent — used in the primary CTA hover gradient, one focal accent per section, and the active state of `CodeArtifact` tabs. Don't add it to the broader palette; keep it as the one identifiable colour. **Why it matters:** Currently NymCard reads as "a blue infrastructure brand"; with one identifiable accent it reads as "NymCard." **Cost:** small (one token, edits to ~5 components). **Order: 4.**

5. **Commission one editorial infrastructure illustration for the nCore section.** The current `NCoreStack` is a 200×400 layer widget; the nCore section deserves the kind of layered architectural illustration that anchors a Stripe product page (`stripe-integrations.png` scale). A bespoke 1200×600 SVG with the six product layers, the nCore engine, the AI fabric threading through, ambient kinetic light. **Why it matters:** nCore is the central proof of NymCard's claim; the visual today undersells it. **Cost:** large (commissioned illustration + bespoke SVG / Framer Motion). **Order: 5.**

6. **Add the missing §8.x primitives: `ScaleStatsRibbon`, `IntegrationsDiagram`, system-level `TrustBar`.** Three primitives the catalogue is missing. Specs already exist in design-system.md (scale stats §9.5, integrations diagram referenced in §3 / §10.2). **Why it matters:** A page-arc compose-from-primitives system that's missing the scale-stats pattern forces a future page to invent it. **Cost:** medium (three components, four-day build). **Order: 6.**

7. **Dial back atmosphere in two homepage middle sections.** Products and one of the cross-link / use-cases sections sit on `surface-soft` with no kinetic ribbon. The peaks (hero, the future scale section, final CTA) gain weight by contrast. **Why it matters:** Currently atmosphere is "always on" — the page reads same-y. Restraint creates rhythm. **Cost:** small (Section-level prop edits). **Order: 7.**

8. **Activate the gradient-bridge transitions between light and dark sections.** The system specs them (§10.3) but the homepage doesn't render any yet. A 40–80px diagonal-slash transition between, say, light Products and the future dark scale-stats section. **Why it matters:** Section transitions become an editorial signature (Stripe pattern) instead of generic stacked blocks. **Cost:** small (one new visual primitive + section integration). **Order: 8.**

9. **Adopt Vercel-style intersection-crosshair markers on the page rails.** Tiny `+` markers where the vertical rails would cross with major horizontal rail lines. Subtle (10px, `text-muted` opacity), but unmistakable as a signature. **Why it matters:** Turns existing rails from passive ambient into active signature treatment. Distinctiveness lever 1 of 2. **Cost:** small (modify the layout-level rails component). **Order: 9.**

10. **Commission three editorial product illustrations to live alongside the product UIs.** Not the UIs themselves — separate, larger conceptual illustrations for the section openers of the eventual product pages (Card Issuing, Lending, Money Movement). Stripe's product-page hero treatments (`stripe-product-home.png`) — that level. **Why it matters:** Product pages are next on the build queue; without illustrations they'll either reuse the homepage UIs (boring) or get placeholders. **Cost:** large (3 commissioned pieces). **Order: 10.**

## What I would NOT change

- **The token system, scale, and spacing values.** They are correctly proportioned, enforced via the `lib/tokens.ts` + `@theme` pipeline, and the radius scale is grep-enforced. Don't touch.
- **The cool-only palette discipline.** It's a real position and worth defending. Just give it a recognisable focal point (recommendation 4) rather than abandoning the discipline.
- **The 22-primitive catalogue's organising principle.** §8.1 → §8.22 is clearly versioned, well-cross-referenced, with reference anchors per primitive. The catalogue itself is good; what's missing is a small set of additions (recommendation 6) and the bespoke UIs that fill the slots.
- **`CodeArtifact`.** This is the best primitive in the system. Leave it.
- **The AI extraction motion spec (§9.5.1).** Don't refine it — ship it on a real product UI.
- **The light + dark dual theme.** Linear-grade work, expensive to redo, not the lever right now.
- **The page rails.** Keep them; consider adding crosshairs (recommendation 9) but don't remove the rails.
- **The navigation system, the floating glass nav, the `Talk to us` CTA placement.** These are working correctly at the system level.

## Open questions for the owner

1. **Typeface license budget?** Söhne is the right institutional move but costs ~$3–8k/year for marketing-site usage. Geist is free but everyone-knows-it's-Geist. GT Eesti Display is mid-cost. Which lane are we in — free + slightly less distinctive, or licensed + truly differentiated?

2. **Illustration / product-UI commission willingness?** Recommendation 1 (six bespoke product UIs) and recommendation 5 (one infrastructure illustration) are the largest quality levers but also the only "large" cost items. Are we in a position to commission this work (in-house design hire, agency engagement, or staffed contractor), or do we need to find a way to ship at Stripe-adjacent quality with current in-house resources? If the latter, recommendation order changes — typeface and accent become #1 and #2.

3. **Dark-section count for the homepage — one or two?** Design-system.md §10.1 leaves this open ("Candidate A: scale stats; Candidate B: nCore deep-dive — decision deferred"). The current homepage has neither — the nCore section sits on `surface-soft` and the scale section doesn't exist as a primitive yet. Recommendation 6 builds the scale-stats primitive — should it ship as dark by default (yes is my recommendation), and should we add the nCore dark-mode variant as well, or commit to one dark section per page?

4. **Distinctiveness moves — pick two of four?** The four candidates in dimension 10 are (a) distinctive typeface, (b) crosshair-marker rails, (c) signature illustration style, (d) AI extraction motion as the brand-defining kinetic moment. Recommendations 1 / 3 / 5 / 9 attack three of these. Which two does the owner want as the locked NymCard signatures — and are there any she'd add that aren't on the list?

## Reference benchmark notes

- **Anthropic — `anthropic.com`** — restraint as composition. Almost no atmosphere, generous spacing, warm neutrals (cream, terracotta, slate), Söhne typography doing most of the editorial work. The "no decoration earns the message" pattern.
- **Anthropic — `anthropic.com/claude`** — the product UI carries the page. Almost no marketing visual; the Claude conversation surface itself is the hero illustration. Worth stealing: the discipline of letting the product image do the entire job.
- **Linear — `linear.app`** — the hero board on dark with the agent / activity column floating beside it (`linear-hero.png`). Single deep purple, single floating-glass overlay, very tight Inter Display tracking. Worth stealing: tracking discipline at hero scale.
- **Linear — `linear.app/method`** — editorial long-form on dark with restrained inline-product moments. Worth stealing: how to make long-form marketing copy read as authoritative documentation.
- **Linear — `linear.app/agents`** — the agents page is the closest live reference to what NymCard wants to feel like for AI-native messaging. Real motion, real agent UI, real product moments.
- **Vercel — `vercel.com`** — Geist + monochrome + the crosshair-intersection markers + literal product-vignette cards. The "infrastructure marketing site that earns its bar with restraint" reference.
- **Vercel — `vercel.com/products/observability`** — the source for `vercel-asymetric.png`. Alternating asymmetric rows; each row is a real-looking product UI vignette (an anomaly chart with a "5xx /api/auth error rate increased to 42%" callout, a root-cause analysis card). Worth stealing: the credibility that comes from rendering UIs that look like the real thing.
- **Stripe — `stripe.com`** — the home for the coral-purple-cyan signature gradient, the angled-page-edge slash, the use-cases rail, the cross-sell banner pattern. The single most-imitated marketing site, and the one NymCard is anchored to.
- **Stripe — `stripe.com/issuing`** — the canonical reference for what NymCard's card-issuing page wants to be. The bespoke card-controls UI, the layered floating dashboard, the per-section product moment.
- **Stripe — `stripe.com/atlas`** — the masterclass in commissioning illustrations for an infrastructure page. Every section opens with an isometric or vector illustration that's bespoke, on-system, and storytelling-first.
- **Stripe — `stripe.com/payments`** — the use-cases rail (`stripe-use-cases.png`) and the connectors diagram (`stripe-integrations.png`) — two patterns NymCard has primitives for or is missing primitives for. Reference these directly when building recommendation 6.
