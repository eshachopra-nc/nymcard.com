# Product Reference — Cards

Source: NymCard Card Issuing MCD. Product truth for the Cards page — don't invent beyond this. Voice: `../../SKILL.md`. Arc: `../page-arc.md`. Platform facts: `../architecture.md`.

## What it is
The card issuer-processing layer of nCore. Banks, fintechs, and digital businesses design, launch, and run card programs on one platform — issuing, processing, authorization, settlement, credit logic, and program operations together. Full-stack card infrastructure, not an issuing API. White-label by default. Can replace a legacy processor, extend a portfolio, or launch a new proposition.

## One-line positioning
Full-stack card infrastructure on nCore — launch and run card programs aligned to your business model, market, and regulatory requirements.

## Capabilities
- **Card infrastructure** — physical, virtual, tokenized cards; full lifecycle (activate, freeze, replace, terminate); real-time authorization; dynamic spend controls.
- **Credit and financing** — consumer, corporate, commercial credit cards; revolving and charge structures; billing cycles; APR and Islamic profit structures; installment plans (BNPL, EPP, balance conversion); delinquency management; rewards and cashback.
- **Program management** — BIN sponsorship, card fulfillment, dispute management, settlement operations, 24/7 operational support.
- **Risk and compliance** — KYC/KYB, AML, sanctions screening, transaction monitoring, 3D Secure, fraud controls (via the Financial Crime layer).
- **Connectivity** — Visa, Mastercard, local rails, wallet and tokenization.
- **Processing foundation** — real-time ledger, multi-currency accounts, authorization and routing, settlement and reconciliation.

## Card program types
Debit, credit, charge, commercial, commercial credit, GPR/prepaid, rewards, gift, youth, BNPL, payout/disbursement, multi-currency.

## Who buys it
Banks, fintechs and digital banks, government agencies, exchange houses, corporate/SME programs, telecoms, retail and marketplaces. Roles: CFO (interchange revenue, vendor cost), CTO/CIO (integration, no lock-in), Head of Cards/Payments (program control, lifecycle), Product (speed, configurability), Risk/Compliance (embedded compliance, monitoring), Operations (reconciliation, disputes).

## How it differs
Full-stack vs fragmented multi-vendor stacks. Native processing on nCore vs external. Credit integrated, not a separate system. Program management and compliance built in, not add-on vendors. Structured migration from legacy processors. Programs can typically go live in under 3 months (qualify: subject to program complexity and market requirements).

## Claims allowed
"Under 3 months go-live" — always qualified. Visa and Mastercard principal membership. PCI DSS Level 1, ISO 27001. Cloud / on-soil / on-premise deployment.

## Don't
Don't position it as just an issuing tool — it's full-stack infrastructure. Don't understate credit. Don't imply a banking license, deposit-taking, or loan funding. Don't claim universal global coverage. No absolute speed claims without the qualifier.

## Cross-sell
Lending, Money Movement.
